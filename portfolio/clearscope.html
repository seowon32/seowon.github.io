ClearScope Surgical Simulator Development

Design & Manufacturing Lead · UT Austin · Jan 2025 – May 2025
Disclosure: This page includes only non-proprietary, public, or personally created content.
Medical device simulation Prototyping DFM / Cost User-centered design

Overview

Developed a realistic laparoscopic surgical simulation platform for ClearCam to demonstrate and validate the performance of the ClearScope laparoscopic lens-cleaning device. The project delivered two simulators: a full-scale human torso simulator and a portable demonstration box to enable controlled debris-based testing and realistic visualization conditions.

Problem

Existing laparoscopic training simulators do not replicate realistic internal debris conditions and often miss key surgical constraints such as trocar insertion, a dark interior environment for visualization, or cost-effective build methods. The sponsor needed a reusable platform that could reliably demonstrate ClearScope effectiveness in a realistic surgical-like environment.

Engineering Objectives

  • Replicate human torso size/geometry with internal cavity access
  • Support trocar insertion points (including triangular placement patterns)
  • Enable tissue-like cutting behavior with replaceable layers
  • Allow controlled internal debris application for repeatable demonstrations
  • Be easy to assemble, clean, and reuse
  • Deliver both a full-scale simulator and a portable demonstration box

My Contributions

Mechanical Design & CAD

  • Led CAD development of the simulator housing and internal structures for modularity and repeatable testing
  • Designed incision and access geometry to support laparoscopic tool and trocar compatibility
  • Optimized parts for manufacturability and assembly

Prototyping & Manufacturing

  • Integrated additive manufacturing with molding workflows to produce structural components and tissue-like layers
  • Developed tissue-mimicking components using silicone/ballistic gel approaches for realistic interaction
  • Designed fastening/reinforcement mechanisms (e.g., mesh + fasteners) to secure replaceable tissue layers

Stakeholder-Driven Iteration

  • Translated sponsor and advisor requirements into engineering specifications
  • Conducted iterative prototyping and incorporated feedback to improve realism, usability, and maintainability
  • Balanced performance, cost, portability, and ease of cleaning for repeated demonstrations

Engineering Approach

  1. Reviewed existing laparoscopic trainer solutions and identified gaps for debris-based demonstration
  2. Converted stakeholder needs into measurable requirements and acceptability criteria
  3. Generated concepts and selected designs using structured evaluation
  4. Built CAD models and prototypes; iterated using material testing and usability feedback
  5. Validated durability, portability, realism, and sponsor usability expectations

Results & Impact

  • Delivered a full-scale torso simulator to create a realistic surgical-like environment for controlled debris testing
  • Designed a portable demonstration box emphasizing collapsibility and transport for sponsor demos
  • Improved the sponsor’s ability to validate device performance and communicate effectiveness to medical professionals
