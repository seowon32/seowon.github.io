Overview

Designed and manufactured a high-performance RC race vehicle from concept to competition. The project focused on balancing structural strength, drivetrain efficiency, manufacturability, and cost constraints.

Design Challenge

Primary Goals
  • Maximize drivetrain efficiency
  • Maintain structural durability under crash loads
  • Achieve competitive speed and acceleration
  • Design for manufacturability using additive processes
Constraints
  • Limited part availability
  • Cost limitations
  • Tight build schedule
  • Weight vs strength tradeoff

System Architecture

The vehicle was composed of four major subsystems: chassis, drivetrain, steering, and electronics. Subsystem integration focused on maintaining belt alignment, load distribution, and steering responsiveness.

  • Belt-driven rear powertrain
  • 3D printed nylon structural chassis
  • Front-wheel parallel steering
  • High-torque brushed motor + ESC

Chassis Design

Material Selection
  • Nylon selected for high strength-to-weight ratio
  • Improved durability vs PLA prototypes
  • Allowed crash resistance without fracture
Manufacturing Optimization
  • Reduced infill to minimize cost
  • Spiderweb rib structure reduced stress fractures
  • FEA performed in SolidWorks to remove excess material

Powertrain Design

Design Strategy
  • Belt transmission for smoother torque delivery
  • Adjustable tensioning system to prevent slip
  • Gear ratio modified during build for torque optimization
Manufacturing Challenges
  • Pulley sourcing forced redesign to 1:9 ratio
  • Custom tensioning rod added
  • New wheel mounting required axle threading

Steering System

Initial Concept
  • Ackerman steering geometry modeled
  • Predicted turning radius: 16.7 cm
Final Implementation
  • Parallel front-wheel steering selected
  • Improved reliability and manufacturability
  • Measured turning radius: 30-40 cm

Testing & Validation

As test lead, I performed track testing, subsystem validation, and failure troubleshooting.

  • Measured acceleration, top speed, braking
  • Validated drivetrain tension and durability
  • Identified steering mounting failures post-race

Manufacturing Decisions

DFM Highlights
  • 3D printed stabilizing components reduced assembly failure
  • Snap and friction fit wheel mounts simplified assembly
  • Material thickness tuned for additive manufacturing reliability
Future Improvements
  • Reduce structural thickness to lower weight
  • Add bolt-adjustable tensioning systems
  • Optimize 3D printing geometry for reliability

Performance Results

  • Top Speed: 4.5 m/s
  • Acceleration: ~3 m/s²
  • Turning Radius: 30–40 cm
  • Completed two races with minor repair required
