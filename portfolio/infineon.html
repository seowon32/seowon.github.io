Overview

During my internship at Infineon Technologies, I worked as an etch manufacturing engineer focusing on process reliability and equipment maintenance strategy. My primary responsibility was analyzing the effectiveness of dummy wafer cleaning processes and expanding their use to improve manufacturing efficiency while maintaining process stability.

Manufacturing Problem

Background
  • Etch chambers accumulate contamination during semiconductor fabrication
  • Dummy wafers are used to clean chambers without risking production wafers
  • Overuse increases cost and machine downtime
  • Underuse increases defect risk and yield loss
Engineering Challenge
  • Determine optimal frequency and conditions for dummy wafer use
  • Balance cost, equipment availability, and yield protection
  • Validate changes using production data

Engineering Approach

  • Collected process data from etch operations
  • Tracked wafer quality and equipment contamination indicators
  • Compared cleaning schedules across multiple runs
  • Developed statistical models to evaluate cleaning effectiveness

This approach ensured recommendations were based on measurable process performance rather than assumptions.

Statistical Analysis Using JMP

Analysis Techniques
  • Regression modeling of process performance vs cleaning frequency
  • Design of Experiments style comparison across cleaning conditions
  • Variance and trend analysis for yield stability
Decision Criteria
  • Maintain defect rates below threshold
  • Minimize cleaning wafer consumption
  • Maximize machine production uptime

Results & Manufacturing Impact

  • Expanded usage strategy of dummy wafers across process steps
  • Reduced unnecessary cleaning cycles
  • Improved machine availability and throughput
  • Supported cost savings through reduced wafer consumption

The project demonstrated how data-driven decisions can optimize semiconductor manufacturing efficiency without compromising product quality.
